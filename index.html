Information: Celengan


Did you know that the history of "piggy bank" can be traced back to Majapahit culture from 13th Century or even earlier?


The Javanese word for "piggy bank" is "celengan", which comes from the root word "celeng", which means "wild boar" in English.


During Majapahit era, it was common to store money or coins in a "celengan", which was located in a common ground.


The boar-shaped (or pig-shaped) bank was seen by some Europeans who visited Majapahit during this era, and was met with worldwide adoption ever since.
